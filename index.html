About Me

I am Qingqing Jiang, a master's student in the School of Earth and Space Sciences at Peking University. My primary research interests include geology, paleoclimatology, and astronomical chronology, with a focus on the Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum (PETM). I am also exploring Earth system models to understand the orbital forcing on climate systems.

Research

My research focuses on the Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum (PETM), investigating its climatic and sedimentological impacts using XRF analysis and astronomical chronology.

  • Sedimentation rates and orbital forcing during the PETM.
  • Earth system modeling using cGENIE to simulate climate impacts.
